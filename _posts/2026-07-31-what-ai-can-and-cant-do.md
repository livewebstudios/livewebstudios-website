## Let's Start With What AI Isn't

AI is not a magic button that spits out a finished, professional website in 30 seconds. If you've seen a demo that says otherwise, that demo left something out. The part where someone who knew what they were doing had to ask the right questions, judge the output, and fix what came back wrong.

"Anyone can build a professional website with AI in an afternoon." In 2026, that's still mostly marketing copy.

## What AI Actually Does Well

**Writing and editing code.** This is where AI has changed professional web development the most. Clean, well-structured code that used to take hours now takes minutes. And that speed gets passed along to you. Faster turnaround, better pricing.

**Generating content drafts.** AI can draft a decent first version of page copy, blog posts, marketing emails. That draft still needs editing and the voice of someone who knows the business. But starting from a draft beats starting from a blank page.

**Iterating on design.** AI tools can spin up layout variations fast and help you decide what works visually. That's a real edge early in a project, when you're still figuring out the direction.

**Pulling insights from data.** Every monthly report we send uses AI to find patterns in your analytics and turn them into plain-English recommendations. That's a practical use that saves time and beats doing it by hand.

## What AI Still Can't Do

**Know your business.** What your website should say, who it's talking to, what action it should drive. That judgment is still a human conversation.

**Replace an experienced eye.** Good web work isn't just code and content. It's knowing what order things go in, what to leave out, what actually turns a visitor into a customer. AI helps with that judgment. It doesn't replace it.
