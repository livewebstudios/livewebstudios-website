<h1>Google Changed the Rules This Summer. Here's What That Means for Your Website.</h1>

<p>If your website hasn't been touched in a year or two, this summer's Google updates are not your friend. Two things happened in quick succession that most small business owners have no idea about. First, Google's March 2026 core update introduced composite Core Web Vitals scoring — meaning your site is no longer graded on individual performance metrics in isolation. Google now aggregates Load Time (LCP), Visual Stability (CLS), and Interaction Speed (INP) into a single composite score. Fail even one and the penalty compounds across all three. Second, AI Overviews — the AI-generated answer blocks that now appear at the top of most search results — don't just rank your page. They decide whether to cite it at all. A page can technically rank on page one and still get passed over if it isn't structured, fast, and authoritative enough to get pulled into the AI summary. That's a new problem that didn't exist 18 months ago.</p>

<p>The practical impact for a local service business — a contractor, an architect, a plumber — is straightforward: slow sites are being penalized harder than before, and static sites that haven't been updated in months are being treated as inactive. Google's freshness signal has always existed, but it's now working alongside AI evaluation that looks at whether your content actually answers the questions your customers are searching. The businesses that show up this summer are the ones with fast-loading sites, regularly updated content, and pages that answer real questions in plain language — not keyword-stuffed pages written for 2018. If you're not sure where your site stands right now, that's exactly the conversation we should have. Call me.</p>
