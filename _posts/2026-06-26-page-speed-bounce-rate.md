## The Number Nobody Likes to Look At

If you have Google Analytics on your site, you probably have a bounce rate. And if your site is running on older infrastructure, that number might make you uncomfortable.

Bounce rate is the percentage of visitors who land on your site and leave without clicking anything. A high bounce rate isn't always a problem — sometimes people find what they need on the first page. But often, especially on mobile, a high bounce rate is telling you something simpler: your site was too slow, and they left.

## What the Research Says

Google's own data has shown that as page load time increases from 1 second to 3 seconds, the probability of a visitor bouncing increases by 32%. From 1 second to 5 seconds, it jumps to 90%. By the time you're at 10 seconds — which some older WordPress sites hit on a mobile connection — the probability of losing that visitor is over 120% higher than a fast site.

That's not a typo. The compounding effect is that dramatic.

## What This Means in Real Numbers

Let's say 500 people visit your site this month. If your site loads in 4 seconds instead of 1, you might be losing 50-100 of those visitors before they ever read a word. If even 10% of those would have become leads, that's 5-10 lost contacts. Every month. For as long as your site stays slow.

## The Fix Is Usually Simpler Than It Sounds

For many small business sites, the path to dramatically faster load times runs through the same place: a lighter, more modern site architecture. No database queries, no plugin stack, no heavy page builder rendering on every load.
