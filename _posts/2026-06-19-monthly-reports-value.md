## The Problem With "Set It and Forget It"

Most website projects end the same way. Launch day, a few excited emails, then silence. The agency moves to the next project. You move back to running your business. Six months later, nobody remembers what the site was supposed to accomplish.

That's not a relationship. That's a transaction.

## What a Monthly Report Changes

A monthly report does a few things at once. It gives you a real window into how your site is performing. Traffic trends, search visibility, your top-performing content. It creates a regular touchpoint that keeps the relationship active. And it builds a record over time. What was working, what changed, what we tried.

The value isn't just the data. It's having someone who's paying attention.

## What Our Reports Include

Every report we send covers:

**Traffic and engagement.** How many people visited, which pages held them, how they moved through the site.

**Search visibility.** Impressions and clicks from Google Search Console, the keywords you're showing up for, and where you're trending up or down.

**Content opportunities.** Two or three specific recommendations from the data. Pages to add, keywords to target, questions your visitors are asking that you're not answering yet.

**What we did this month.** A short plain-English rundown of the actual work completed.

**What's coming next.** So you always know what we're focused on.

## Why It Works as a Retention Tool

When clients see steady evidence that their site is being maintained and improved, they don't leave. It's that simple. The monthly report isn't just a document. It's proof of ongoing value.
