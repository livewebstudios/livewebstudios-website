## The Most Common Objection I Hear

"I don't have anything to write about." I hear it from architects, contractors, attorneys, landscapers. Basically every small business owner I work with. And I get the instinct. You're an expert in your field, not a content creator.

But here's the thing. Your expertise is the whole point of the blog.

## How Your Customers Actually Find You

Before most people hire anyone, they Google a question. Not "best plumber in [city]." Something more specific. "Why is my water pressure low?" "How long does a water heater last?" "Do I need a permit for a bathroom renovation?" These are pre-purchase questions, and people search them thousands of times a month.

A blog post that answers one of those questions puts your name in front of someone at the exact moment they realize they might need help. That's a very different thing than a yellow pages ad.

## You Don't Have to Write It Yourself

This is where modern tools help. We use AI writing tools in our workflow, and they make a real difference. You provide the expertise. What do your customers ask? What do people get wrong about your industry? What should someone know before they hire you?

We turn those answers into SEO-optimized posts that sound like you. Because they're built from what you know.

## How Often Do You Need to Post?

Consistency beats frequency. One solid post a month, every month, beats five posts in a burst and then nothing for half a year. Google rewards regular, fresh content.

## The Practical Result

Over 12 months, even a modest posting habit builds real authority on your topics. Your site starts showing up for more searches. More searches, more visitors. More visitors, more leads.
