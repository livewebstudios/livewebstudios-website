## Most Analytics Dashboards Show You Too Much

Google Analytics 4 is powerful. It's also overwhelming if nobody ever trained you on it. Most business owners open the dashboard once, see a wall of numbers, and close the tab. I don't blame them.

So here's the five-minute version. The numbers you actually need, and what they mean.

## Users and Sessions

**Users** are the individual people who visited your site in a given stretch of time. **Sessions** are the individual visits. One user can rack up several sessions. Visit Tuesday, come back Thursday, that's one user and two sessions.

For most small business sites, the trend matters more than the raw number. Is traffic growing month over month? That's the whole question.

## Engagement Rate

GA4 retired the old "bounce rate" and gave us engagement rate instead. It's the share of sessions where someone actually did something. Scrolled, clicked, stuck around more than 10 seconds. A higher number means people are finding your site useful. Under 50%? Go look at why.

## Top Pages

Which pages pull the most traffic? If your contact page lands in the top five, that's a very good sign. And if a blog post you wrote six months ago is still driving real traffic, that's proof your content marketing is doing its job.

## Traffic Sources

Where are these visitors coming from? Organic search means your SEO is paying off. Direct means people are typing your URL or hitting a bookmark. Referral means another site is sending them. Social means your posts are getting clicks.

Your traffic mix tells you what's working and where to put your next dollar.

## That's It

Sessions, engagement, top pages, traffic sources. Four numbers. The rest of GA4 has its uses once you know the context. But those four tell you whether your website is working.
