## The Word "Migration" Makes People Nervous

It shouldn't. In practice, migrating a straightforward small business site to a modern stack is closer to a renovation than a move. Your address doesn't change. Your content stays. Your visitors don't notice the transition. What changes is what's happening under the hood.

Here's how we actually do it.

## Step 1: Audit and Scope

Before any code gets written, we look at what exists. What pages does your current site have? What functionality does it use — forms, a blog, any custom features? Are there things that need updating along the way? This conversation takes about 30 minutes and shapes everything that follows.

## Step 2: Design Carryover

We're not redesigning from scratch. We're translating what you have into a cleaner, faster version of itself. If there are improvements to make — and there usually are — we make them now, not later.

## Step 3: Build

Using Claude Code and Cursor, we build the new site in a clean modern codebase. This is where the AI tools do the heavy lifting on the technical side. We're reviewing, directing, and quality-checking throughout.

## Step 4: Content Review

You get to look at the staging version before anything goes live. If something's off, we fix it. This isn't a "hope they don't notice" process.

## Step 5: Launch

The new site deploys through Netlify. The DNS update is the last step, and it's usually invisible to visitors — there's no downtime.

## What You Have to Do

Review the staging version. Give feedback. That's it. We handle the rest.

## Timeline

For a typical 5-10 page small business site: about a week of build time, a day or two of review, and a clean launch.
