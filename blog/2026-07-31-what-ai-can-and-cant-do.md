## Let's Start With What AI Isn't

AI is not a magic button that produces a perfect, finished, professional website in 30 seconds. If you've seen demos that suggest otherwise, those demos are leaving out the part where someone with real expertise had to know what to ask for, how to evaluate the output, and how to fix the parts that weren't right.

The promise of "anyone can build a professional website with AI in an afternoon" is, at this point in 2026, still mostly marketing copy.

## What AI Actually Does Well

**Writing and editing code.** This is where AI has had the most dramatic impact on professional web development. Writing clean, well-structured code that used to take hours now takes minutes. That speed advantage gets passed along in the form of faster project turnaround and more competitive pricing.

**Generating content drafts.** AI can draft a reasonable first version of page copy, blog posts, and marketing emails. A good first draft still needs editing and the voice of someone who actually knows the business — but starting from a draft is faster than starting from nothing.

**Iterating on design.** AI tools can rapidly generate layout variations, try different approaches, and help make decisions about what works visually. This is especially powerful in the early stages of a project.

**Generating data insights.** Every monthly report we send uses AI to pull patterns out of your analytics and translate them into plain-English recommendations. That's a real, practical use case that saves time and produces better output than a manual process.

## What AI Still Can't Do

**Know your business.** The strategic judgment about what your website should say, who it's talking to, and what action it should drive — that's still a human conversation.

**Replace an experienced eye.** Good web work isn't just code and content. It's knowing what order to put things in, what to leave out, and what actually converts visitors into customers. AI assists that judgment; it doesn't replace it.
