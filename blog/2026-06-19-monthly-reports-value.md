## The Problem With "Set It and Forget It"

Most website projects end the same way: launch day, a few excited emails, and then silence. Your agency moves to the next project. You move back to running your business. Six months later, nobody can remember what the site was supposed to accomplish.

That's not a relationship. That's a transaction.

## What a Monthly Report Changes

When we send a monthly report, we're doing a few things at once. We're giving you a window into how your site is actually performing — traffic trends, search visibility, top-performing content. We're creating a regular touchpoint that keeps the relationship active. And we're creating a record: what was working, what changed, what we tried.

For a business owner, the value isn't just the data. It's having someone who's paying attention.

## What Our Reports Include

Every report we send covers:

**Traffic and engagement** — how many people visited, which pages they spent time on, and how they behaved.

**Search visibility** — impressions and clicks from Google Search Console, which keywords you're showing up for, and where you're trending up or down.

**Content opportunities** — two or three specific recommendations based on the data: pages to add, keywords to target, questions your visitors are asking that you're not answering yet.

**What we did this month** — a short plain-English summary of actual work completed.

**What's coming next** — so you always know what we're focused on.

## Why It Works as a Retention Tool

When clients see consistent evidence that their site is being actively maintained and improved, they don't leave. It's that simple. The monthly report isn't just a document — it's proof of ongoing value.
