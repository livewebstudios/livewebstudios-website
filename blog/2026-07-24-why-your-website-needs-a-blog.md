## The Most Common Objection I Hear

"I don't have anything to write about." I hear this from architects, contractors, attorneys, landscapers — basically every small business owner I work with. And I understand the instinct. You're an expert in your field, not a content creator.

But here's the thing: your expertise is exactly what the blog is for.

## How Your Customers Actually Find You

Before most people hire anyone for anything, they Google a question. Not "best plumber in [city]" — something more specific. "Why is my water pressure low?" "How long does a water heater last?" "Do I need a permit for a bathroom renovation?" These are pre-purchase questions, and they're being searched thousands of times per month.

A blog post that answers one of those questions puts your name in front of someone at the exact moment they're realizing they might need professional help. That's a very different kind of marketing than a yellow pages ad.

## You Don't Have to Write It Yourself

This is where modern tools — including AI writing tools we use in our workflow — can make a real difference. You provide the expertise: what are the questions your customers ask? What do people misunderstand about your industry? What should someone know before they hire a contractor, or an architect, or a law firm?

We can turn those answers into well-written, SEO-optimized blog posts that sound like you, because they're built from what you know.

## How Often Do You Need to Post?

Consistency beats frequency. One solid post per month, published consistently, outperforms five posts published in one burst and then nothing for six months. Google rewards regular, fresh content.

## The Practical Result

Over 12 months, even a modest blogging cadence builds topical authority. Your site starts showing up for more searches. More searches mean more visitors. More visitors mean more leads.
