## Most Analytics Dashboards Show You Too Much

Google Analytics 4 is genuinely powerful. It's also genuinely overwhelming if you haven't been trained on it. Most business owners who open their analytics dashboard for the first time see a wall of numbers and close the tab.

Here's the five-minute version: the numbers you actually need to understand, and what they mean.

## Users and Sessions

**Users** are individual people who visited your site in a given time period. **Sessions** are individual visits. One user can have multiple sessions (if they visit Tuesday and again Thursday, that's one user, two sessions).

For most small business sites, watch the trend over time more than the absolute number. Is traffic growing month over month? That's the question.

## Engagement Rate

GA4 replaced the old "bounce rate" with engagement rate — the percentage of sessions where someone actually interacted with your site (scrolled, clicked, spent more than 10 seconds). A higher engagement rate means people are finding your site useful. Under 50% is worth investigating.

## Top Pages

Which pages are getting the most traffic? If your contact page is in the top five, that's a very good sign. If a blog post you wrote six months ago is driving significant traffic, that's evidence that content marketing is working for you.

## Traffic Sources

Where are your visitors coming from? Organic search (Google) means your SEO is producing results. Direct traffic means people are typing your URL or clicking a bookmark. Referral means another site is sending you visitors. Social means your social posts are driving clicks.

Knowing your traffic mix tells you what's working and where to invest.

## That's It

Sessions, engagement, top pages, traffic sources. Four things. Everything else in GA4 is useful in context — but those four numbers tell you whether your website is working.
